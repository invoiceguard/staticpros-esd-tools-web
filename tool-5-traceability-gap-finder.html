⚠️ Why This Matters

Many "calibration" certificates lack true NIST traceability. Use this checker to identify gaps that could fail your audit. Look at your certificates and answer honestly.

1 Does your certificate show an ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation number?

🚨 CRITICAL GAP

Without ISO 17025 accreditation, your certificates may not be accepted by auditors. The lab must be accredited by an ILAC MRA signatory (A2LA, ANAB, etc.).

2 Is there a NIST traceability statement?

🚨 CRITICAL GAP

ANSI/ESD S20.20 requires NIST-specific traceability. Generic "national standards" claims are insufficient for defense/aerospace compliance.

3 Does it show measurement uncertainty?

⚠️ MODERATE GAP

ISO 17025 requires documented measurement uncertainty. Without it, you can't properly assess compliance risk (pass/fail could be in the noise).

4 Are calibration dates and intervals listed?

⚠️ MODERATE GAP

Valid certificates must show when calibration was performed and when it expires. An "issue date" without calibration data could mean it's just a template.

5 Does it list the actual equipment tested?

⚠️ MODERATE GAP

ANSI/ESD S20.20 requires traceability to specific equipment. Generic certificates that don't identify what was tested are not compliant.
